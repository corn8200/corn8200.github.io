The six rules that decide everything

start here

The whole system is an extraction-shooter loop bolted onto Battle Royale. Learn these six and the rest is detail.

Rule 1 · Banking

A sprite is worthless until you extract it

Find it, level it, and it's still a temporary rental. Extraction is the only save point. Epic

Rule 2 · Loss

Die holding it and it's gone — and stealable

Elimination drops your equipped sprite and every spare you were carrying on the ground, levels intact, for the next player to bank. Epic

Rule 3 · Persistence

Extraction saves the level, permanently

Extract at Level 3 → it comes back at Level 3 next match. This was genuinely contested online; Epic's own help article settles it. Epic

Rule 4 · The catch

Buying a lost sprite back always returns it at Level 1

Even if it's Mastered. Epic: "Mastering a Sprite doesn't mean their level won't reset after losing them." Mastery only unlocks rewards and Pod styles. Epic

Rule 5 · Mastery

Level 5 alone does nothing

You must extract at Level 5 to Master a sprite. Reaching max and dying banks nothing at all. Epic

Rule 6 · The free win

A Victory Royale auto-extracts everything

Win the match and every sprite on you banks for free — no site, no crate, no risk. Data

The one-sentence strategy

Because banked level is the only level that survives, the correct loop is extract early and extract often — every level you gain since your last extraction is money on the table in a game where anyone can take it off your corpse. Hoarding a Level 4 Mythic until the endgame is how most players lose their season.

Variants

four live, three in the files

A variant keeps the base sprite's full ability and adds its bonus on top — Epic's words: "20% more Ammo on top of their Sprite power." They level and Master exactly like base sprites, each is its own collection entry, and only the one in your dedicated slot does anything. Epic deliberately calls the bonuses "(small)" — this is a collection chase, not a power spike.

Two things nobody tells you about variants

They cost 2–40× more to re-summon. A Gold Water Sprite is 4,000 Dust against 100 for the base — so losing a variant hurts far more than losing a base sprite. Special summon costs are flat per tier: 4,000 Rare / 6,000 Epic / 10,000 Legendary / 15,000 Mythic.

Three sprites have no variants at all: Burnt Peanut, Vini Jr. and Pollo. If a checklist tells you to hunt a Gold Burnt Peanut, that checklist is wrong.

Direct source conflict, unresolved: Epic says Holofoil is "slightly easier to find than Gold." The datamined loot tables say Holofoil is the rarest variant by roughly 3×. Both are documented; nobody has reconciled them.

The extraction clock

where matches are lost

There are exactly three ways to bank a sprite: an Extraction Crate called at one of the 17 Extraction Sites, a Portable Extractor gizmo used anywhere, or winning the match. The crate is the one with rules people get wrong.

45s rift-in 1:10 open window →10s 30s teardown
45s rift-inYou hit the screen, the crate flies in. Loud, beaconed, visible on the map. Free bait for anyone nearby.
1:10 openThe crate accepts sprites from any player — including the enemy who just killed you.
→ 10s collapseThe instant anyone deposits one sprite, the remaining window slams to ten seconds. This is the squad-wipe trap.
30s cooldownThen the site is callable again. Sites are reusable, not one-shot per match.
  • The squad trap. Because the first deposit cuts the window to 10 seconds, everyone must be stacked and holding the sprite they want banked before the first person touches the crate. One eager teammate extracting early costs the rest of the squad their sprites.
  • The quickbar trap. Interact with a crate while not holding a sprite and it auto-extracts the first sprite in your quickbar, reading left to right. This is exactly how people bank a junk Level 1 instead of the Mythic they've been protecting.
  • Anyone can use a crate anyone called. Two techniques fall out of that: third-party a site around 40 seconds into the call animation and take the crate the caller paid for — and conversely, if you're losing the fight at your own crate, the enemy inherits it.
  • Sites are dead until the first Storm phase forms. Both the crate screen and the Dust terminal are non-interactable before that. Hot-dropping an Extraction Site for an early bank is wasted time.
  • The beacon is free intel. Red beacon = pre-first-storm. Blue = live and callable. No beacon inside a live circle = someone already called it, or it's cooling down. Somebody is there, or was, very recently.
  • Call a crate even with nothing to bank. Since v41.20, Extraction Crates can also contain Shield Bubble Jr, Crash Pad Jr, Chug Splash or Friend Zone Bubble. The 17 sites are general-purpose loot points now.

Sprite Dust: the only thing that survives your own death

Dust carries between matches, can't be dropped, and is not lost on elimination. Cap is 200,000. Extraction pays from 500, scaling with rarity and level (Gummy variants +20%).

How much is a full Level 1 → 5, really?

Epic has never published the thresholds, no wiki has them, no dataminer has them, and — contrary to what a lot of guides imply — the in-game level bar shows no numbers at all. It's a graphical bar with a "Lv. N" plate.

So we measured it: two creators' 1080p runs, HUD bar de-slanted into strips, fill fraction read before and after XP awards of known size. Result: roughly 350 / 590 / ~1,000 / ~1,150 XP for levels 1→2 through 4→5, i.e. ~3,000–3,200 XP total. That's about 41–43 containers or ~16 eliminations from scratch — halved to ~21 containers on a Mastery Monday. Est. ±10–15%

Two useful corollaries: a full max in a single match is entirely realistic, and surplus XP carries over into the next level rather than being discarded — so banking a big elimination right before a level boundary isn't wasted.

  • Quack Snacks are a full instant level. 1,500 Gold Bars at Mending Machines (the medic-cross ones). They level the equipped sprite, so swap your target in before eating one — and two of them is the difference between a useless Punk Sprite and an unlimited-ammo Punk Sprite.
  • Do not farm bots for XP. Bot lobbies pay a flat quarter: containers 19 instead of 75, eliminations 50 instead of 200. The advice to "land quiet for easy bot kills" is actively backwards.
  • Mastery Monday is the leverage window. Monday 9am ET → Tuesday 9am ET: 2× Sprite XP, 2× Sprite Dust from extractions, and boosted Legendary/Mythic spawn rates. Four remain: 27 Jul, 3 Aug, 10 Aug, 17 Aug.
  • Dream Sprite can't be Mastered normally. At Level 5 it explodes with Legendary loot, force-extracts itself and resets to Level 1. It's the one sprite that is completely immune to die-and-lose-it.
  • A sprite handed to you keeps its level. Levels ride with the sprite instance, not the player — which is the basis of the whole trading economy, and the single most useful mechanic for playing with a kid.

Finding them

four rails

The map is Shattered Coast. Sprite Chests are the primary rail, but three others matter — and one patch on 16 July changed the maths completely.

The most important change of the season

v41.20 rewrote Sprite Chest loot. Epic, verbatim: "An Epic, Legendary or Mythic Sprite, or a higher-rarity variant is guaranteed. Sprite variants now appear more frequently. Rare Sprites will drop less often, and only as variants." Epic

Practical effect: Sprite Chests are now strictly better than normal chests, every drop-rate table written before 16 July is void, and since Dust payout scales with rarity, your income per extraction went up too.

Rail 1

Sprite Chests

Blue glow, pink crystal on the lid, a chest-audio cue like Chapter 2 vaults. Fixed candidate spawn points, randomly activated each match — a marked spot is a possibility, not a guarantee.

Turn on Visualized Sounds. It converts the chest audio into a direction indicator on your HUD. Highest-leverage settings change for farming, by a distance.

Rail 2

Wild roaming sprites

They spawn by biome, and this is the best-documented routing data anywhere: Water wet areas · Fire urban · Earth forest · Air mountains · Punk sewers/underground · Duck around Dirty Duck's · Seven forests, mountains, the Zero Point · Vini Jr. the Soccer Field · Ghost the whole island, at night only.

Chest-only (no roaming route): Fishy, Demon, King, Aura, Dream, Burnt Peanut.

Rail 3

Named routes

Boss Sprite — kill Wolfe, The Voidblade or Skeletor. Striker — score a goal at the Soccer Field. Batman — Hot Bat Summer quests, or duel Coastal Catwoman / Poison Ivy / High Tide Harley. Vaults — small keyed vaults each hold a Sprite Chest; two Epic-keycard "Sprite Vaults" hold several. Fishing works too.

Rail 4

Gizmos

Sprite Locator (v41.20) spawns a buried chest with a random sprite. Hotfixed today (21 Jul) to strongly favour variants — and Epic granted 3 free ones to everyone who'd used an extraction gizmo, acknowledging it underperformed.

Lucky Locator — guarantees a sprite you haven't collected. Not live. Deferred to v41.30, and it's the single best collection tool coming.

  • Per-POI chest counts are genuinely unreliable. The widely-copied "33 total" figure traces to one article from 9 June. A July-updated census sums to ~62 across 23 POIs while its own headline still says 33. Treat any specific per-POI number as approximate — including "Sinister Strip has the most."
  • Farm in Zero Build. Rotations are faster, you're moving between chest spawns instead of harvesting mats, and central-map POIs let you chain more spawns per circle.
  • Killing runners is a legitimate farming strategy. Players carry up to five spare sprites plus their equipped one, all of which drop with levels intact. A player camping an extraction site is a piñata.
  • Buy the 100-Dust "Locate Sprites" scan. One extraction pays 500+, so it's effectively free, and almost nobody uses it.
  • No NPC sells sprites. All 11 NPCs were checked. The closest thing is Heatwave PJ at Latte Landing (350 gold) who pings chests and enemies in a wide radius.

Which ones are actually good

consensus across 8 lists

One structural insight explains the entire tier list better than any ranking: sprites are ranked by activation cost, not by rarity or raw numbers. Every consensus top pick triggers off something you already do involuntarily — healing, eliminating, reloading, traversing terrain, simply existing. Every trap demands a deliberate detour or a level gate that outlasts the average match.

Consensus #1

Zero Point

Auto-spawns a Shield Bubble Jr. when you heal yourself. Top of every list found. In Zero Build it's arguably the best in the game — it erases the mode's biggest vulnerability, healing in the open.

But: at 0.00093% from chests it's effectively unfarmable. If you want it, you summon it for 7,500 Dust.

Consensus #2 · best aggressive

Demon

Siphons health and shield on every elimination, ~10 → ~30 effective HP by level. Top-tier in every source without exception. Best solo pick in the game — it's self-sustain with no teammate dependency, and it directly counters being third-partied.

Highest skill expression

Ghost

Cloak on reload, 3s → 5s. The S/A swing pick — the split is about skill floor, not power. Its value is conditional on you deliberately reloading to trigger it.

Counterplay to know: cloak still leaves footsteps and a shimmer, and Seven Sprite's foot-trail reveal reads straight through it.

Newest top-3 entrant

Boss

Flat +HP and +Shield, up to roughly +25 each. The only pure raw-stat sprite: no activation condition, no skill requirement, no positioning. Ranked top-3 in the only tier list that postdates the 16 July patch.

Trap #1

King

Bonus pickaxe damage. The only sprite Epic has explicitly nerfed (v41.10, against players and environmental objects), and it sits bottom in every single list. No source recommends it for any playstyle.

Trap #2

Water

Shield regen while in water. Asks you to stand in water — positionally suicidal, and often simply impossible if the final circle is land-heavy.

Trap #3 · conditional

Dream & Punk

Both are Legendary sprites whose entire value sits behind Level 5, which most matches end before. Punk does literally nothing until Level 5 — then always grants unlimited ammo.

Genuine bait for casuals; a real win condition for grinders with two Quack Snacks.

Rarity trap

Grim

Mythic rarity, B-tier performance. Marks anyone who damages you — but Fortnite's existing damage indicators and audio already tell you roughly where the shot came from.

Important scope limit

None of this exists in Ranked

Epic, verbatim, on the extraction gizmos: "Available only in unranked Battle Royale and Zero Build matches." Sprites, Sprite Dust and Extraction Sites all carry the wiki's not-in-Competitive banner, where "Competitive" links directly to the Ranked article.

Sprites also appear in no other mode — not Reload, OG, Blitz, Ballistic, Save the World, Festival, Rocket Racing, or Creative. Battle Royale Build and Zero Build only.

A caution on tier lists

Most of what you'll find is stale

Eight independent tier lists exist. Only one postdates the 16 July patch — so Batman, Air, Seven, Vini Jr. and Pollo have essentially no consensus yet. Any list that doesn't include Striker, Fishy, Aura, Boss and Grim predates 25 June and is missing a third of the roster.

Also worth knowing: that single recent list is a 1–21 ranking, not letter tiers. Several sites have "converted" it into S/A/B/C tiers that its author never wrote.

Things good players know

and casuals don't
  • Aura + Shock Rocks is the season's cleanest engineered combo — and it only became reliable on 16 July, when Epic made shield damage count toward the charge. Before that you had to break shields before the sprite paid out at all.
  • You can Shock Rock–jump while knocked. A downed teammate can launch themselves toward cover or a reboot instead of crawling. Shock Rocks can also be thrown, granting the effect to everyone in the blast radius.
  • Seven Sliders overheat in ~7.5 seconds on the ground — but sliding on water cools them. ADS bullet-time mid-slide also reduces heat buildup, so you can chain a far longer slide than the default.
  • Vini Jr. is built specifically around Seven Sliders. Sprinting makes your slide destructive; slide-kicking enemies buffs fire rate and reload speed. The two items are designed as a package.
  • Zero Point's bubble blocks bullets, not bodies. You beat it by walking through the dome and shotgunning, by destroying the 400 HP centre prop, or with a Shield Breaker EMP — not by shooting the bubble.
  • Battle Bus Victory Royale: one teammate aboard wins it for the whole team — including teammates who are knocked or fully eliminated. It spawns inside the 6th storm circle with a 2-minute window, and multiple teams can win the same match. The supply drops around it carry Friend Zone Bubbles: a 30-second dome that blocks fire from both directions, which is the single best tool for holding the bus.
  • Safe Extraction is the anomaly to drop everything for. It wraps every in-circle Extraction Site in a bubble that blocks all gunfire and prevents players inside from damaging each other. Extraction becomes completely non-lethal. In Zero Build it also hands out unlimited Pocket Shockwave Grenades.
  • Storm Surge fights the whole season's incentive structure. The sprite economy pulls you toward avoiding fights to protect an unbanked sprite; Surge punishes exactly that. The resolution good players use: bank early with a Portable Extractor, then play normally.
  • Spare sprites cost you real inventory slots for zero benefit. A player hauling three spares to a site is fighting with a two-slot loadout — and holding a sprite in your hands prevents weapon use entirely.

Playing with kids

the parts that bite
The good news

You can hand a leveled sprite to a kid

Carry it as an inventory item, drop it on the ground, they pick it up. The receiver inherits its level — so a Level 4 you grinded becomes their Level 4, and if they extract it, they bank both the sprite and the progress.

The cost

Your copy resets to Level 1

Epic counts dropping as "losing" it. And you can only re-summon with Dust if you had already extracted that sprite at least once — give away a sprite you've never banked and it's gone for good.

Split-screen

PlayStation and Xbox only

Not Switch, not mobile, not PC — and not in Solos, Ranked, or Creative. Player 2 links their own Epic account, so their collection and Dust are genuinely theirs.

The catch: P2 never gets a lobby, so P2 cannot equip a sprite or spend Dust to summon one before the drop. Menu inventory sharing is also blocked — but ground drop/pickup still works, which is exactly the handoff mechanic above.

Bot lobbies

Roughly 17 of 21 sprites are unreachable

Since 8 June, bot lobbies only carry Water, Fire, Earth and King. Sprite XP is quartered. And kids on Switch or mobile are matched into bot-heavy lobbies by platform, so the restriction hits them hardest.

The upside: bots basically never win, so Victory Royales — and therefore free auto-extractions — come easily.

Mobile bug, still open

A custom HUD can silently break extraction

A custom layout without a Reload button, or with Dynamic Quickbar Slots on, can make Portable Extractors completely unusable. Fix: re-add the dedicated Reload button and use the default quickbar. Still unfixed as of Epic's latest revision.

Handoff timing

The 10-second collapse is brutal with a slow kid

The moment the first person deposits, everyone else has ten seconds. Get the kid to the crate first, holding the right sprite, before anyone touches it.

Advice that's already dead

the fact-check pile

This season moves weekly, and a lot of very confident guides — including some updated today — are running stale or invented numbers. These are the ones worth knowing about.

  • "Light a campfire for double Sprite XP." Dead. It was a Gone Wild event modifier, enabled 25 June and switched off 9 July. Late-June leveling guides still lead with it.
  • "Throw or drop sprites into the crate." Patched 30 June — it was a duplication glitch. Walk up and interact.
  • "Farm bots for easy sprite XP." Backwards. Bot lobbies pay a flat quarter across every action.
  • "Gummy is +10% Dust, Galaxy is +20% ammo." Both stale since 19 June — they're 20% and 30% now.
  • Any drop-rate table written before 16 July. One widely-cited table's percentages sum to 202%, because it pairs current variant rates with pre-patch base rates. Another site's figures are a full order of magnitude off a third's. Nobody has Epic numbers, because Epic has never published any.
  • "Burnt Peanut is the only sprite with no variants." Three have none: Burnt Peanut, Vini Jr. and Pollo.
  • "Extracted sprites come back at Level 1." Only Dust-summoned copies do. Genuinely extracted ones return at the level you banked them.
  • "There's a John Wick Sprite." Datamined only, placeholder icon, no ability, not in the Battle Pass. Do not put it on a checklist.

How this was built. Nine parallel researchers covered the roster, variants, acquisition, the extraction economy, leveling, tier-list consensus, patch history, advanced play and mode scoping. Every claim then went to an adversarial fact-checker instructed to refute it — which killed a fabricated tier-list conversion affecting 13 claims, several invented drop rates, a stale campfire strategy, and a "source conflict" on level persistence that turned out to be two articles describing different cases.
