Getting Your Gun Rights Back

A step-by-step plan to legally erase a 20-year-old felony — and put you back in the deer woods with your own rifle.

Yes. This is fixable.

West Virginia has a law written for exactly your situation: one old, non-violent felony, sentence finished long ago, clean ever since. It's called expungement — a judge orders the conviction erased, and the law treats it as if it never happened. When that order is signed, your right to own a gun comes back under both West Virginia law and federal law. Your record comes clean for everything else, too.

Why you qualify

  • Your offense is eligible. Buying/selling stolen goods is a property offense. West Virginia's expungement law has a list of crimes that can never be expunged — violent crimes, sex offenses, DUI, crimes against kids. Yours is not on it.
  • One felony is exactly what the law allows. The statute covers a single non-violent felony conviction.
  • The waiting period is long gone. You only have to wait 5 years after the sentence ends. You've waited roughly 20.
  • Sentence complete, nothing pending. That's the last box, and you check it.

WV Code § 61-11-26 (non-violent felony expungement, expanded 2019).

The plan, step by step

Six steps. John handles the paperwork and phone calls; your part is mostly telling the truth and signing your name.

1

Find the old case file John's job

Everything files in the county where the conviction happened, so we need the case number and certified copies of the old paperwork. Here's a head start: we already searched West Virginia's online court system — all 55 counties, every circuit-court criminal record back to 1999 — and your case isn't in it. That means it's almost certainly from before 1999, sitting in a clerk's paper index. Clerks look these up by phone all the time; the law makes them keep felony files for 75 years, so it's there.

First call — Cabell County (Milton's county): Circuit Clerk, 750 Fifth Ave, Room 114, Huntington — 304-526-8622, weekdays 8:30–4:30. Backup — Greenbrier County (home): 304-647-6626, Lewisburg. Give your full name and date of birth every time — more than one Jimmy Cornelius has come through Frankford, and the birth date keeps the clerk on the right man. Certified copies are $1 a page ($5–15 for the packet): we need the indictment and the sentencing order.

If neither clerk finds it: a West Virginia State Police record check on yourself (a fingerprint card and a small fee) lists every WV conviction with its exact court, county, and case number — the guaranteed answer. And if that search shows the case was actually a misdemeanor, that's the best news possible — misdemeanor theft never took your gun rights in the first place, and the State Police printout would be your proof.

2

Read the file for two things Together

First: the paperwork must not say a weapon was “used or exhibited” during the offense — that's the one wrinkle that could block expungement of a property crime. Second: any old fines and court costs need to be paid off (if anything's outstanding, we pay it — it's usually small). This weekend, John also needs the straight story: which county, roughly what year, and that there's nothing else on your record anywhere — no other convictions, nothing domestic-violence related, nothing pending. No judgment; the plan just has to be built on the real record.

3

Fill out the official forms John drafts · You sign

The state publishes the exact forms free: petition SCA-C907 with instruction packet SCA-C900 (courtswv.gov). John fills them out; the main written part is the story of the last 20 years — steady work, family, hunting heritage, staying clean. You read it, make sure it's true, and sign in front of a notary (free at your bank).

If money's tight, one extra form (SCA-C&M-201, a financial affidavit) asks the court to waive the $200 filing fee entirely.

4

File it and mail the copies John's job

File at the circuit clerk's office in the conviction county — $200, or $0 with the fee waiver. The instructions list the offices that get copies (State Police, the prosecutor, the arresting agency, the court) — certified mail, done in an afternoon.

5

Wait out the clock Nobody's job

Anyone who objects has 30 days to say so. Then the court must act within 60 days of filing — the judge can grant it on the papers alone, or set a short hearing. If there's a hearing, you show up, dress plain, and tell the truth: twenty-plus clean years speaks for itself. The legal standard is showing you're rehabilitated — and you've been living the proof for two decades.

6

Order granted — lock in the proof Together

When the judge signs: pay the $100 State Police processing fee, and immediately order 4–5 certified copies of the expungement order. That sounds backwards, but once the record is sealed, the order itself is your proof it happened — you'll want copies for gun purchases and one for the truck glovebox, forever.

One important note: West Virginia gives each person this relief once in a lifetime. That's why we do it carefully and right the first time — real records, honest paperwork, every box checked. Done that way, your case is about as strong as they come.

This fall — already legal, starting now

  • Archery & crossbow deer season: Sept 26 – Dec 31, 2026. Bows and crossbows are not “firearms” under federal or West Virginia law — a felony conviction doesn't touch them. You can buy a bow tomorrow and hunt the whole season, fully legal.
  • Big-bore air rifle. WV allows air rifles .45-cal and up (200-grain bullet minimum) for deer during firearms seasons — and air rifles aren't legally firearms either. A modern PCP air gun drops deer clean inside 75 yards.
  • Muzzleloaders — not yet. Oddly, the law technically excludes old-style muzzleloaders too, but there's a trap (modern inline primers count as banned “ammunition”), and it's not worth any risk months before the real fix. Wait for the order, then hunt every season there is.

Until the judge signs — the don't list

These keep the plan safe. A few months of discipline, then it's over.

  • No guns and no ammunition under your control — the ammo part surprises people: a single loose shotgun shell in the truck is its own federal felony.
  • “Not mine” doesn't help if it's in your space. Guns in your house, your truck, or a camp you control can be charged as yours. If others in the household own guns, they stay locked up, someone else keeps the key.
  • Don't hold, borrow, or haul anyone's gun — even carrying a buddy's rifle back to the truck counts as possession.
  • At family hunts, you're the bow guy this year. Next year you're not.

After the order — your first rifle in 20 years

With the expungement in hand, the law treats the conviction as if it never happened. At the gun counter you answer “No” to the felony question on the federal form, honestly. One heads-up: the FBI background-check computers can take a while to catch up with a fresh expungement, so the first purchase may come back delayed or even wrongly denied. That's normal and fixable, not a problem — the dealer can release after 3 business days on a delay, and a certified copy of your order wins any appeal. There's even a one-time FBI registration (a “UPIN” number) that makes every check after that come back clean and instant. Once you're restored, West Virginia's permitless concealed-carry applies to you like anyone else.

The timeline

  • By roughly Halloween–Thanksgiving — the court's 60-day deadline forces a decision.
  • Best case — you're buying your own deer rifle before the late seasons. Worst case, spring gobblers 2027 with your own shotgun. Either way, bow season is yours the whole time.

If Plan A ever hits a wall

Two backups exist, and your profile is strong for both. West Virginia also lets you petition your home-county court (right in Lewisburg) just for gun rights — but that one has a catch: it fixes state law only, and federal courts have held it doesn't clear the federal ban on its own. It's a supplement, never the main move. And in 2025 the federal government revived its own rights-restoration program for the first time in 33 years — a $20 application expected to open during 2026, and the example case their own rulebook describes is basically you: a decades-old, non-violent felony and a clean life since. We'd only need it if the state path failed.
