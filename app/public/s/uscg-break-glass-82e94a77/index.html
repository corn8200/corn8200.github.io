Break Glass — flip only if stuck

Recovery lines · scripted curveballs · acronym decoder · facts you might misquote

Blanked? Buy 10 seconds — any of these

  • "Let me make sure I answer the right question — are you asking about [A] or [B]?"
  • "Good question — let me think for a second." (Pausing reads as senior. Sip water. Write the question on your pad.)
  • "Here's what I know firsthand, and here's where my knowledge ends…" (then the pivot →)
  • Total blank on a term → just ask: "How does your shop use that term? I want to answer your version of it, not the textbook's."

The pivot — for anything you haven't done

1. Name the gap precisely (no bluffing) → 2. name the adjacent thing you HAVE shipped → 3. say how you'd close it.

"I haven't [X]. The closest I've done is [Y] — same pattern, different wrapper. First week, I'd sit with whoever owns [X] here and learn your version before building anything."

Scripted curveballs

  • "What salary are you looking for?" → "I'm confident we can sort pay-setting out at the offer stage with HR — I'd rather spend our time on the work."
  • "Why leave a corporate job that surely pays more?" → "Twenty years in uniform — mission still matters to me. This is fifteen minutes from my house, it's the exact intersection of what I build now and what I did in the Army, and it's rare to see a role this precisely aimed at what I do." (Never mention money pressure, leave, or health.)
  • "When can you start?" → "A professional transition for my employer — two to three weeks after a final offer."
  • "Interviewing elsewhere?" → "I have other federal irons in the fire, but this is the role that matches what I actually do."
  • "Clearance status?" → "Held TS/SCI for years — SSBI/T5. Inactive since my 2022 retirement, reinstatable on sponsorship."
  • "This role may require a cert (Sec+/CISSP tier)." → "Give me the qualification target and a date and I'll meet it. I've never missed a standard — I used to enforce them for a living."
  • "Can we contact your supervisor?" → "My current chain doesn't know I'm looking — contact me first. Julie Weissenberger can speak to my prior TAMKO roles."
  • "What about prompt injection?" (CISSP bait) → "Someone hides instructions inside a document the model retrieves. Defenses: retrieved text is treated as data, never instructions; the corpus only admits authoritative, version-controlled docs — nobody can slip an arbitrary file in; output is constrained to draft-and-cite; and a human validates anything consequential."

Whiteboard panic — two safe drawings

RAG: [docs]→[chunk]→[embed]→(VECTOR DB) …library, built once [question]→[embed]→top-5→"answer from these + cite"→human
VSM: AS-IS : human finds evidence → reads all docs → writes SSP → weeks per system, no audit trail TO-BE : pipeline collects evidence → RAG drafts w/ citations → ISSO validates & owns → hours, fully auditable

Narrate while drawing — silence at a whiteboard reads worse than a plain diagram. Start with AS-IS; ask THEM to correct it: instant working session.

Real-world emergencies

  • Running late / gate trouble: call Pete's cell 717-377-1693 the moment it's possible you'll miss 15:00. One apology, no story.
  • Forgot resumes: "I'll email the current version before close of business" — then do it from the truck.
  • Bombed a question: circle back at the end: "I want a second pass at your earlier question — the better answer is…" Recovering out loud is a strength.
