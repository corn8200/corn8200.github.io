Do tonight — before you sleep

Book 4 free zoo entry passes. Every person needs their own — including James & Jude. It's a hard gate; don't gamble on walk-ups with two little kids. Weekday = better odds.
nationalzoo.si.edu/passes
Buy the MARC train tickets — CharmPass app. Download CharmPass (MTA's official app) and buy 2 adult tickets each way — $10 one-way each, $40 round-trip for two. Kids ride free (up to 2 under age 6 per adult), so don't buy tickets for James & Jude. Activate each pair right before you board — outbound at Brunswick, return at Union Station (activating starts the clock). Backup: card machines at both stations. Don't buy onboard — it adds a $5 surcharge.
Get a real SmarTrip ready — for the DC Metro. Separate ticket from the train: the subway needs a SmarTrip card in Apple Wallet (or plastic) — DC gates don't reliably take a bare credit-card tap. Jude rides free (under 5, up to 2 kids per adult); if James is 5+, he needs a fare too.
Pack the bag. Water bottles (refill stations inside the zoo), light rain jackets / stroller cover, sunscreen, snacks. Bring the stroller — wagons rent inside, wheelchairs are free.

Inside the zoo — know before you go

The internal shuttle is NOT running right now. There's no ride back up the hill. Either pace the climb back to the entrance, or turn around before you reach the very bottom. A rented wagon saves little legs.
Pandas are at the top by the entrance — keep left. See them first at 10, no backtracking.
The zoo is built on a hill: enter at top, walk down, then walk back up to leave. Plan the day downhill-first.
Open 8am–6pm (buildings 9–6). Your 10–2:30 window has hours of margin — zoo says 2–3 hrs to see it all.
Water refill stations throughout. Strollers/wagons rent at the Visitor Center; wheelchairs are free.
