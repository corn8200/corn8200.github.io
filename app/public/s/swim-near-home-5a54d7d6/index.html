Lake beaches

6 spots

Guarded sand beaches on clear mountain lakes. The two closest (Greenbrier, Cacapon) fill up on hot weekends — reserve and arrive early.

Greenbrier State Park

◈ Boonsboro, MD

~45 min

White-sand swim beach on the spring-fed, mountain-ringed 42-acre Lake Greenbrier, with canoe and kayak rentals. The closest guarded lake beach to home.

CostSummer $3/person weekdays, $5 weekends & holidays (WV plates add $2/person).
SeasonBeach mid-May–mid-Sept; lifeguards 11am–6pm Memorial Day–Labor Day.
Heads upAdvance day-use reservation required on summer weekends/holidays (parkreservations.maryland.gov). Fills to capacity — reserve and arrive before 10am.

Before you go — a few honest cautions

The rivers, creeks, and swimming holes here have no lifeguards and can carry currents; check Swim Guide for the latest water-quality reading and skip natural water for ~3 days after heavy rain. One specific warning: the Harpers Ferry two-river confluence is not a safe swim spot — the Park Service strongly discourages it due to sinkholes, strainers, and rapids. And Lake Arrowhead in Luray is out — the town permanently closed its swim beach in 2025. Fees, hours, and drought advisories shift mid-season, so a quick call ahead saves a wasted drive.
